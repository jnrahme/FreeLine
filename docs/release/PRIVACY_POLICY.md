# FreeLine Privacy Policy

Draft only. Review with counsel before publishing.

Effective date: `[TO_BE_FILLED]`

FreeLine (`[LEGAL_ENTITY_NAME]`, `we`, `our`, `us`) provides a U.S.-only app-based calling and texting service that lets a user claim one FreeLine number and communicate over Wi-Fi or mobile data. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and the choices available to users.

## 1. Information We Collect

We may collect:

- account information such as email address and authentication provider identifiers
- device and security information such as device identifiers, app version, operating system, IP address, session metadata, abuse-risk signals, and push tokens
- FreeLine number information such as area code searches, assigned number metadata, activation status, reclaim status, and quarantine history
- communications data such as message content, message timestamps, call timestamps, call duration, call status, voicemail metadata, and voicemail recordings
- support and moderation information such as spam reports, blocks, abuse reviews, account suspensions, and support tickets
- monetization data such as subscription state, rewarded-ad unlock events, ad impressions, and ad clicks

We do not market FreeLine as a guaranteed number for verification or one-time passwords, and emergency calling is not provided through the FreeLine app flow.

## 2. How We Use Information

We use information to:

- create and secure accounts
- assign, manage, and reclaim FreeLine numbers
- deliver calling, texting, voicemail, and notifications
- enforce fair-use, anti-spam, anti-fraud, and abuse controls
- comply with telecom, legal, and platform obligations
- operate ads, subscriptions, and usage unlocks
- diagnose failures, improve reliability, and support users

## 3. Sharing

We may share information with:

- telecom providers used to provision numbers, route calls, route texts, and process related events
- service providers that help with push delivery, hosting, storage, analytics, subscriptions, support, and advertising
- law enforcement, regulators, or other parties when required by law, legal process, or to protect users, our systems, or the public
- a successor entity if the business is merged, sold, or reorganized

We do not sell message content or voicemail recordings as standalone data products.

## 4. Retention

We retain data for as long as reasonably necessary to:

- operate the service
- investigate fraud or abuse
- enforce telecom compliance rules
- resolve disputes
- meet legal retention requirements

Free numbers may be reclaimed after inactivity under the product rules, and historical records may be retained after a number is released.

## 5. User Choices

Users may:

- update account access methods where supported
- release a number from inside the app
- block and report conversations
- request account deletion by contacting `[SUPPORT_EMAIL]`

Deletion requests may be subject to records we must retain for abuse prevention, financial reporting, or legal compliance.

## 6. Security

We use reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect data, but no system is perfectly secure. Users are responsible for keeping their own devices, email accounts, and authentication methods secure.

## 7. Children

FreeLine is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly allow such users to operate accounts.

## 8. U.S.-Only Service

FreeLine is designed for U.S. use only. Number inventory, calling, texting, and abuse rules are built around U.S. telecom requirements and may not function outside that scope.

## 9. Changes

We may update this Privacy Policy. If we make material changes, we will update the effective date and provide notice as required by law or platform policy.
